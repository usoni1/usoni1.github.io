Utkarsh Soni

I am a Computer Science Ph.D. student at the School of Computing and Augmented Intelligence, Arizona State University. I work in the Yochan research group directed by Prof Subbarao Kambhampati. My current research focuses on personalizing the interaction between a human and an AI agent. More specifically, I look at how an agent can generate personalized explanations for sequential decision making problems and how to enable a user to specify their preferences to an reinforcement learning agent that operates on some inscrutable representation of the task like images.

Prior to my PhD, I completed my M.S. in Computer Science at ASU, where I worked in the Vader Lab under Prof Ross Maciejewski on graphical layout perception. I also worked on an urban employment tool meant for government decision makers to help them analyze multiple aspects of employment data in their region.
